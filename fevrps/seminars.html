This webpage presents the current research results of the Berlin University Alliance X-Student Research Groups on the topics "How can we design systems for intelligent vehicles?" and "How can we model and solve challenges for future mobility applications?".

The overarching aim of our courses is to support students in the transition between their current undergraduate or graduate studies and their future postgraduate or industry careers by engaging them with a number of current challenges in the area of mobility.

For this reason, the webpage includes a portfolio of interesting research topics with information such as topic descriptions, research questions, working groups, and their current findings and publications.

In addition, the webpage includes details on publishing opportunities such as conferences, funding opportunities such as grants, and participation opportunities such as webinars. Finally, we look forward to welcoming you to our courses!

Topic 1: Dial-A-Ride Dispatching for Emergency Medical Services (Emergency Services)

Topic description

Eranus Telebus Berlin (https://www.eranus-telebus-zentrale-berlin.com/) promotes transportation services to people with mobility hardships. The project focuses on optimizing such services for patients with different levels of emergency between home and hospital locations in Berlin, accounting for emergency preferences such as how long time patients would like to wait for the arrival of ambulances and by what time patients would like to arrive at hospitals, as well as availability constraints such as respecting ambulance capacities, driver shifts, and hospital capacities.

Research question

How can we optimize the transportation of home patients to hospital locations using ambulances while respecting emergency preferences and availability constraints?

Topic 2: Intelligent Garbage Collection by using Internet-of-Things Sensors (Garbage Collection)

Topic description

Saubermacher, Nordsense, Sensoneo, and Mr.FILL use Internet-of-Things Sensors for detecting the level of garbage in containers across industrial markets such as construction sites and concert events. The project focuses on transferring this idea to household markets in Berlin and other German cities for the on-demand collection of garbage from residential areas. This requires predicting the garbage level and scheduling the collection trucks dynamically, subject to balancing the associated workload of collection teams and reducing the associated carbon footprint of trucks.

Research question

How can we dynamically predict the level of garbage and schedule the garbage trucks for collecting this garbage while balancing the workload of collectors and reducing the carbon footprint?

Topic 3: Data-Driven Drone Navigation for Fighting Wildfires (Drone Navigation)

Topic description

The bush wildfires in Australia in 2019-2020 damaged nearly 60-84 million acres of farmland, left approximately 3 500 people without homes, and impacted more than 3 billion animal lives. Fire departments often struggle to re-allocate their firefighters properly due to the low visibility from the ground and the limited budget for helicopters. The project investigates the deployment of drones for improving the allocation of firefighters dynamically by extending their visibility and, thus, navigating them toward areas of greatest risk.

Research question

How can we deploy the minimum number of drones for maximizing area coverage and informe the allocation of firefighters by estimating the risk imposed by wildfires while respecting the available budget?

Topic 4: Minimizing Response Time of Relief Vehicles after Natural Disasters (Relief Vehicles)

Topic description

2004 Indian Ocean earthquake and tsunami took more than 230 000 human lives. Minimizing the response time of relief support units could be crucial for saving at least some part of human lives at locations hit by such natural disasters. The project focuses on optimizing the routing of relief support vehicles for the delivery within given deadlines of various critical supplies such as medicine and food to locations affected by disasters, subject to accessibility of locations and capacities of vehicles.

Research question

How can we optimize the arrival of relief support units to affected areas while accounting for different vehicle types and location accessibility?

Topic 5: Taxi Dispatching under Multi-layer Preferences (Taxi Dispatching)

Topic description

Mobility is not gender-neutral because women travel more often and use more modes of travel than men in their daily routines. As a result, women are more vulnerable to sexual assaults in public transport than men which forces them to use alternative means of travel such as booking taxis more often. The project investigates optimizing the matching between taxi drivers and taxi passengers, subject to two-layer driver preferences for trip profitability and route popularity, as well as two-layer passenger preferences for trip fare and service satisfaction.

Research question

How can we optimize the matching between taxi drivers and passengers while accounting for the multi-layer preferences of drivers and passengers?

Topic 6: Ride Sharing under Multi-dimensional Preferences (Ride Sharing)

Topic description

Ride-sharing has the potential to help us achieve the goals of the European Green Deal by promoting strategies for say shared commuting such as the adoption of 2+ road lanes implemented say by Navitel Israel. For example, it is estimated that around 90 000 people use their private cars for daily commuting in Berlin. Sharing those cars would deliver a significant reduction in the carbon footprint associated with them. The project studies maximizing the number of people sharing rides, subject to limiting the individual cost and time for sharing rides.

Research question

How can we maximize the ride-sharing efficiency while accounting for individual preferences such as reducing the cost and time for people when sharing rides?

Topic 7: Ship Routing Avoiding Whale Migration Paths (Ship Routing)

Topic description

More than 20 000 whales are killed every year as a result of ship transportation near their migration paths. The project focuses on designing routes for ships, subject to avoiding the migration paths of whales and, thus, minimizing the potential danger ships could cause to them. This involves the frequent monitoring of whale locations and possible re-routing of the original paths of the ships. Additional factors that play a role in the design require meeting the shipping deadline for the cargo and respecting the available capacities of the docks.

Research question

How can we compute shortest ship routes while avoiding whale migration paths, respecting dock available capacities, and meeting cargo shipping deadlines?

Topic 8: Modeling Commuting Preferences for Microtransit Services (Microtransit Services)

Topic description

Microtransit services are on-demand shuttle services that facilitate access to public transport for residents in detached areas. The project aims at modeling the preferences of commuters in Berlin for Microtransit services such as BVG Muva and Berlkönig. Such preferences could relate to how much time cummuters would like to walk at most to Microtransit stops, how much money they could pay for the service, and how many accompanying persons they want to use the service with. These could inform the existing services about the construction of new stops and the need for more drivers.

Research question

How can we evaluate the commuting demand for Microtransit services in the Berlin area while preserving service affordability and maximizing service satisfaction?
